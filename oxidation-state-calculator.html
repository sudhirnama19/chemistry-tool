Oxidation States: Tracking Electron Flow

The Oxidation State (or oxidation number) is a theoretical value assigned to an atom in a compound, representing the number of electrons lost or gained by that atom relative to its neutral state.

$\sum (\text{Oxidation States}) = \text{Total Charge of Molecule/Ion}$

This concept is the bedrock of **Redox Chemistry**, allowing scientists to identify which species are oxidized (lose electrons) and which are reduced (gain electrons) during a chemical transformation.

The 7 Fundamental Rules of Oxidation Numbers
  • Elemental State: The oxidation state of any pure element (e.g., $O_2, Fe, S_8$) is always $0$.
  • Monatomic Ions: The state equals the charge (e.g., $Na^+$ is $+1$, $Cl^-$ is $-1$).
  • Fluorine: Always $-1$ in compounds because it is the most electronegative element.
  • Oxygen: Usually $-2$, except in peroxides ($-1$) or with Fluorine ($+2$).
  • Hydrogen: Usually $+1$ with non-metals, but $-1$ with metals (hydrides).
  • Group 1 & 2: Group 1 metals are always $+1$; Group 2 metals are always $+2$.
  • Summation Rule: The sum of all states must equal the overall charge of the species.
Step-by-Step Solved Examples

Example 1: Sulfur in $SO_4^{2-}$
1. Oxygen is $-2$. There are 4 oxygens: $4 \times (-2) = -8$.
2. Let $S = X$. The net charge is $-2$.
3. Equation: $X + (-8) = -2 \rightarrow X = +6$.
**Oxidation state of S is +6.**

Example 2: Carbon in $C_2O_4^{2-}$ (Oxalate)
1. $2(C) + 4(-2) = -2$.
2. $2C - 8 = -2 \rightarrow 2C = 6 \rightarrow C = +3$.
**Oxidation state of C is +3.**

Applications in Scientific Research

Battery Technology: In Lithium-ion research, the oxidation state of Cobalt or Manganese changes during charge/discharge cycles. Measuring these states helps engineers increase battery longevity.

Metabolic Biochemistry: The Nitrogen cycle relies on bacteria changing Nitrogen's oxidation state from $-3$ (Ammonia) to $+5$ (Nitrate), a process vital for all life on Earth.
