The Matrix Solver Method

Unlike simple trial-and-error, this balancer uses Linear Algebra. Every chemical reaction is a system of equations where each element must satisfy:

$$ \mathbf{A} \vec{x} = \vec{0} $$

Where $A$ is the element matrix and $x$ is the vector of coefficients.

This approach allows for the solving of complex redox reactions, such as the decomposition of nitrogen triiodide or the oxidation of potassium permanganate, which are impossible to solve by inspection.
