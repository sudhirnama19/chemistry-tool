Guide: Entering Values

Our calculators use a smartParser to interpret scientific notation and standard math symbols.

^
Exponent: Use 10^1 for powers (10 to the power of 1).
*
Multiply: Use 10*1 to multiply values together.
e
Scientific: 1.8e-5 is the standard shorthand for $1.8 \times 10^{-5}$.

Practical Examples:

  • Positive Exponents: Enter 10^2 for 100.
  • Negative Integers: Enter -5 for acidic/logarithmic scales.
  • Complex Powers: Enter 10^-7 for neutral pH water calculations.
