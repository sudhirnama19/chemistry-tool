Reaction Rate & Collision Theory Expert Guide

The Collision Theory states that for a reaction to occur, particles must collide with 1) Correct Orientation and 2) Sufficient Energy (Activation Energy, $E_a$).

The Frequency Factor (A)

The Arrhenius equation $k = Ae^{-E_a/RT}$ includes the pre-exponential factor A, which represents the frequency of collisions with the correct orientation.

Arrhenius Equation: ln(k) = ln(A) - (Ea / RT)

Temperature Coefficient ($Q_{10}$) Trick

In most biological and chemical systems, the rate constant increases by a factor of 2 to 3 for every 10 Kelvin rise in temperature. This is known as the $Q_{10}$ temperature coefficient.

FAQ

What is an Effective Collision?

A collision that results in a chemical reaction. The fraction of effective collisions is given by $f = e^{-E_a/RT}$.

How does temperature affect collisions?

Higher temperature increases both the frequency of collisions and the kinetic energy of particles, significantly increasing the number of particles that cross the $E_a$ threshold.
