What is Molarity?

Molarity, also known as molar concentration, is a measure of the concentration of a chemical species, in particular of a solute in a solution, in terms of amount of substance per unit volume of solution. In competitive chemistry (GATE/CSIR-NET), Molarity is crucial because it relates the volume of a solution directly to the stoichiometry of a reaction.

Pro Tip: Molarity is temperature-dependent because liquid volume changes with temperature, whereas Molality ($m$) remains constant.

Definition

Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute dissolved in exactly one liter ($1\text{ L}$) of the solution. The standard unit is moles per liter ($mol/L$), often denoted by the capital letter M.

Calculated Examples (JEE/GATE Level)

Example 1: Basic NEET Level
Calculate the molarity of a solution containing $4\text{ g}$ of $NaOH$ in $250\text{ mL}$ of solution.
Solution: Moles of $NaOH = 4/40 = 0.1$. Volume $= 0.25\text{ L}$. $M = 0.1 / 0.25 = \mathbf{0.4\text{ M}}$.
Example 2: Advanced Density-Based
A sample of $H_2SO_4$ is $98\%$ by mass with a density of $1.84\text{ g/mL}$. Find its molarity.
Formula: $M = \frac{\% \times d \times 10}{M_{solute}} = \frac{98 \times 1.84 \times 10}{98} = \mathbf{18.4\text{ M}}$.
