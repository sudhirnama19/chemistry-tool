Molarity (M) Definition

Molarity is the number of moles of solute dissolved in one liter of solution. It is a temperature-dependent concentration unit.

Advanced Example: For a sample of $H_2SO_4$ that is $98\%$ by mass with a density of $1.84\text{ g/mL}$, the formula is: $$M = \frac{\% \times \text{Density} \times 10}{M_{\text{solute}}}$$ Result: $\mathbf{18.4\text{ M}}$.
