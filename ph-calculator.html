Understanding pH

The pH of a solution measures its acidity or basicity. It is defined as the negative logarithm of hydrogen ion concentration.

$$\text{pH} = -\log[H^+]$$

For basic solutions, we first calculate $pOH$ and then use the relationship: $$\text{pH} + \text{pOH} = 14$$

Expert FAQ

1. Why is pH 7 neutral?
At 25°C, pure water auto-ionizes into equal amounts of $H^+$ and $OH^-$ ($10^{-7}\text{ M}$ each).

2. Can pH be negative?
Yes, for highly concentrated strong acids (molarity > 1.0), the pH can drop below 0.
