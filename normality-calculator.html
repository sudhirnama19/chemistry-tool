Fundamental Normality ($N$)

Normality is the number of gram-equivalents of solute dissolved per liter of solution. Unlike Molarity, it accounts for the reactive capacity of the species.

$N = \frac{\text{Mass (g)}}{\text{Eq. Wt} \times \text{Vol (L)}}$
$\text{Eq. Wt} = \frac{\text{Molar Mass}}{\text{n-factor}}$

Advanced Research & Exams

JEE Advanced & GATE: Focuses on "Double Titrations" and Back-Titration where $N$ simplifies calculations by using the law: $N_1V_1 = N_2V_2$ without needing balanced stoichiometric coefficients.

Unique Fact: Normality is **temperature-dependent** because it is based on volume. In high-precision CSIR-NET research, Molality is preferred for thermal stability.

Frequently Asked Questions
Can Normality be less than Molarity?
No. Since $n$-factor is $\ge 1$, $N$ is always $\ge M$.
What is the n-factor of $KMnO_4$?
It depends on the medium: 5 (Acidic), 3 (Neutral), and 1 (Strongly Alkaline).
